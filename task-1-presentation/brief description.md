Description: For my Task 1 deliverable, I put together this Digital Literacy Essentials infographic. Honestly, I wanted to take the classic, old-school advice we all grew up with—like "don't talk to strangers" and "keep a neat notebook"—and translate it for our hyper-digital, forward-thinking era.

The graphic breaks down into three no-nonsense sections. First, it defines what digital literacy actually means in the real world. Second, it highlights the absolute core tools any student needs to survive the academic grind: Evernote for keeping notes structured, Google Calendar for strict time management, and Zoom for knocking out group projects. Finally, it covers the non-negotiable safety rules. Just like locking your front door at night, you have to lock down your online presence with strong passwords, dodge sketchy phishing links, and protect your personal info.
