SOCIAL MEDIA DO'S AND DON'TS FOR COLLEGE STUDENTS

THE 5 DO'S 

Do treat your profile like a living resume. Your old-school reputation matters just as much in the digital age, so keep it clean for future employers./n
Do lock down your privacy settings. Keep your private life private—some traditions of boundaries never go out of style./n
Do fact-check before you share. We're forward-thinking scholars, not out here spreading baseless rumors./n
Do network purposefully. Use platforms to actually build your future and connect with industry veterans./n
Do take a breath before replying. If a post triggers you, step away. The drafts folder is your absolute best friend./n

THE 5 DON'TS

Don't post anything you wouldn't want your grandparents to see. Seriously, no sugar-coating it: the internet is forever./n
Don't badmouth your university, professors, or peers. Have some traditional respect; screenshots exist and word travels ridiculously fast./n
Don't overshare your real-time location. Basic safety first—don't broadcast exactly where you are to the whole grid./n
Don't engage in random comment wars. It's a massive waste of energy and just leaves a messy digital footprint./n
Don't rely on "vanishing" messages. People screenshot everything. Assume absolutely nothing is truly temporary./n
