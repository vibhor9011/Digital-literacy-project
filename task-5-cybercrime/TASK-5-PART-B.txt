PART B-PREVENTION CHECKLIST

STAY SAFE ONLINE: THE COLLEGE SURVIVAL CHECKLIST

Real talk: Your UPI PIN is ONLY for sending money, never for receiving. If someone tells you to enter your PIN to "receive" a payment, they are 100% trying to scam you. No cap.

Always double-check the VPA name before hitting 'Pay'. If the name on the screen doesn't match the person you're actually trying to pay, cancel the transaction immediately. It’s better to be safe than broke.

Stop doing your banking on free campus or cafe Wi-Fi. It is way too easy for hackers to peep your info on public networks. Use your own data or a trusted VPN for anything involving money or passwords.

If a friend DMs you begging for urgent cash, give them an old-school phone call first. Accounts get hacked every day, and a quick 30-second call can save you from sending money to a total stranger.

Turn on 2FA (Two-Factor Authentication) for your main accounts. It takes an extra five seconds to log in, but that second layer of security is actually clutch for keeping hackers out of your business.

Use a password manager and stop recycling the same password. Using your birthday or "Password123" for your university portal and your Insta is basically leaving your front door wide open.

Ignore those "Your account is blocked" or "Your parcel is stuck" panic texts. If the link looks sus, it is. Don't click it—just go directly to the official app or website to check.

Be smart about your digital footprint. The internet is forever, and recruiters definitely check your socials. Don't post anything today that you’ll have to explain away in a job interview five years from now.
