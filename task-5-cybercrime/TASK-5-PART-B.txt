CASE STUDY: UPI "RECEIVE MONEY" FRAUD
UPI fraud is a major cybercrime in India where scammers manipulate victims into authorizing money transfers from their own bank accounts. Let's look at the classic "Collect Request" scam, a trap many college students fall into when selling second-hand textbooks or bikes on platforms like OLX.
Here is the step-by-step breakdown of how this crime happens:
    1. The Setup: The student lists an item for sale online.
    2. The Bait: A scammer reaches out immediately, acting eager to buy the item at full price without any traditional haggling.
    3. The Trap: To "secure" the purchase, the scammer insists on sending the money via UPI immediately.
    4. The Execution: Instead of transferring funds, the scammer sends a "Collect Request" to the student's UPI app.
    5. The Pressure: The scammer calls the student, using high-pressure tactics to rush them into entering their UPI PIN, falsely claiming it is required to "receive the funds."
The typical targets are college students, young professionals, or senior citizens—basically anyone rushing through a transaction who forgets the golden rule: a UPI PIN is strictly used to deduct money, never to receive it.
The consequences are brutal and immediate financial loss. Because the victim actively authorized the transaction with their own PIN, banks consider it a legitimate payment and usually cannot reverse the charges. It's a harsh reminder that the old-school logic of "if it sounds too good to be true, it probably is" absolutely applies to modern tech.
